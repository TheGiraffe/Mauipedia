<p>
Kihei is a town of about 21,000 people on Maui that is known for its nice beaches and sunny weathers, and is a popular tourist destination.
</p>
<h2>Notable Places</h2>
<p>
Notable places include Kalama Park (which has both the skate park and the skate rink), Kihei Aquatic Center, the Cove, and other beaches such as Keawakapu, Charlie Young, and the Kam 1-3 beaches and parks.
</p>

<h2>Festivals</h2>
<p>Kihei hosts the Maui Whale Festival annually in February or March at Kalama Park, which includes a Whale Day parade, live music, food, bouncy castles, and other activities for keiki and adults alike.</p>
<h2>
Schools
</h2>
<p>
Kihei has several elementary schools: Kihei Elementary, Kamali'i Elementary, and Kihei Charter. Middle Schools include Lokelani Intermediate and Kihei Charter. The only public high school in Kihei at the time of this writing is Kihei Charter School, a public charter school.
</p>
<h2>
Research and Tech
</h2>
<p>
Research facilities in Kihei include the Hawaiian Islands Humpback Whale National Marine Sanctuary and the Maui Research, DEKALB Genetics Corporation and Monsanto Company, and Technology Park, which has a super computer [Maui High Performance Computing Center (MHPCC)]. 
</p>
<p>
MRTP is home to other businesses and organizations such as Pacific Disaster Center, Maui Economic Development Board, Goodfellow Bros Inc, Boeing, Cohana Coworking Space, Maui Innovation Group, and Pacific Defense Solutions.
</p>
